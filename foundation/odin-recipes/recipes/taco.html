Glorious Tacos

Description

Behold, the humble taco: three crisp shells, folded with the precision of a valet parking a Bentley, cradling a riot of spiced beef mince that’s been persuaded into submission with cumin, chilli, and a whisper of garlic. Beneath lies a treacherous pool of molten cheese, oozing like gossip at a village fête, while shredded lettuce provides a crisp, green alibi for health. Atop it all, a jaunty crown of diced tomatoes—scarlet, pert, and utterly convinced of their own importance—perches with the smugness of a cat who’s knocked your vase off the mantel. The whole affair steams like a trio of smug volcanoes, quietly plotting to ruin your shirt and your diet in one delicious eruption.

Ingredients

  • 3 crisp taco shells (the sumg upright kind)
  • 500g beef mince (spiced until it blushes)
  • 1 sachet of taco seasoning (the lazy chef's secret handshake)
  • Handful of grated cheddar cheese (melts faster than your resolve)
  • Shredded iceburg lettuce (green confetti for that guilt free crunch)
  • 2 ripe tomoatoes, diced (jaunty red pom-poms)
  • A splash of lager (optional, for the chef)

Steps

  1. Brown the beef mince in a hot pan until it’s crisply obedient, then stir in the taco seasoning with a splash of water; let it simmer like a sulky teenager for 5 minutes.
  2. Warm the taco shells in a low oven (or microwave if you’re in a hurry) until they’re toasty but not yet plotting rebellion.
  3. Spoon the spiced beef into each shell, dividing it with the fairness of a Tory MP sharing austerity.
  4. Blanket the beef with grated cheddar—generously, as if cheese were going out of fashion.
  5. Scatter shredded lettuce over the cheese like green ticker-tape at a very restrained parade.
  6. Crown each taco with diced tomatoes, arranging them with the smug precision of a window dresser.
  7. Serve immediately, before the volcanoes erupt and your shirt becomes a casualty.
